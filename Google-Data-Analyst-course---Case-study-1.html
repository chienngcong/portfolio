Case study: Cyclistic How to tranform the casual riders to manual riders

Part 1: Ask

  1. Business task statement As a junior data analyst at Cyclistic, my task is to analyze how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently. The goal is to uncover behavioral patterns and trends in ride length, frequency, day-of-week usage, and other relevant variables. These insights will support the marketing team in designing targeted strategies to convert more casual riders into annual members and thus increase long-term revenue for the company.

  2. My stakeholders:

  • Lily Moreno - Director of Marketing needs insights to guide the new marketing campaign
  • Marketing Analytics Team - Will use your analysis to measure performance and impact of the campaign
  • Cyclistic Executive Team - Final decision-makers who must approve any proposed strategy

  1. The business context There are over 5800 bikes have been shared and over 692 docking stations. The users are devided two type: Annual members and casual riders (single/day-pass users). Depening on their purposes, but the large scale in their users uses bike for their daily traveling to work. There are 4 types of bike: Standard bikes, e-bikes, hand tricycles, cargo bikes (8% use assistive options)

  2. The business questions

  • How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?
  • Why would casual riders choose to become members?
  • How can digital media be used to influence their decision?

Conclusion

  1. Launch Targeted Weekend Membership Promotions Insight: Casual riders mostly ride on weekends and have longer ride durations, indicating leisure-oriented behavior. Action: Develop a Weekend Explorer Membership a low-cost, short-term plan (e.g., valid only on Saturdays and Sundays) promoted via mobile notifications and email after a casual ride on a weekend. This taps directly into their behavior and encourages habitual use.

  2. Trigger Smart Notifications After Ride Thresholds Insight: Casual riders already interact with the system multiple times before considering membership. Action: Implement automated in-app messages or emails after the 2nd or 3rd casual ride, highlighting the cost savings and benefits of becoming a member. Include a comparison: Youve already spent $X this week a membership couldve saved you Y%.

  3. Create Personalized Onboarding for Casual Riders Insight: Casual users are diverse some explore the city, others exercise. Action: After a casual riders first trip, ask one quick question via app or email: What do you use our bikes for? Based on the response (e.g., leisure, fitness, commuting), deliver custom benefits messaging tied to their habits e.g., fitness perks, commuter pass incentives, or scenic route tips all while nudging toward membership.
